\section{Overview}\label{overview}

This file constitutes the \textbf{first-stage (PSU) area sampling frame}
for the Detroit Transportation Security Survey. It lists all census
tracts in the City of Detroit that meet the minimum eligibility
threshold for inclusion as primary sampling units (PSUs) in the
multi-stage area probability design.

We constructed the frame from two sources: (1) a list of Detroit census
tract FIPS codes obtained from the City of Detroit Open Data Portal, and
(2) tract-level counts from the 2020 Decennial Census Demographic and
Housing Characteristics (DHC) Summary File (table P12), accessed via the
\texttt{tidycensus} R package. We excluded tracts with zero adults aged
18 and older and tracts with fewer than 50 adults because they do not
constitute viable household survey PSUs.

\section{Notes}\label{notes}

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\item
  \textbf{Source:} 2020 Decennial Census DHC Summary File, table P12
  (Sex by Age), accessed via the \texttt{tidycensus} R package for Wayne
  County, MI, and filtered to Detroit census tract FIPS codes from the
  City of Detroit Open Data Portal.
\item
  \textbf{Eligibility:} This file lists only the operational tract
  frame. In the updated source data, we started from 276 deduplicated
  Detroit tracts and removed 10 tracts with fewer than 50 adults aged
  18+ before finalizing the frame.
\item
  \textbf{Composite MOS:} The composite MOS is used for
  probability-proportional-to-size (PPS) selection at Stage 1. It is
  constructed to produce equal expected field workload across selected
  tracts while meeting the domain-specific respondent targets. Higher
  MOS values indicate tracts with larger effective sampling burden under
  the age-differentiated design.
\item
  \textbf{Block group count:} The \texttt{No.\ BGs} column reflects the
  number of eligible block groups associated with each tract in the
  block-group-level frame (FRAME2\_NEYMAN.csv). Tracts with a single
  block group have that block group selected with certainty at Stage 2.
\item
  \textbf{Seed:} The sample was drawn using \texttt{set.seed(-77)}
\end{enumerate}
